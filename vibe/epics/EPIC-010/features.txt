EPIC-010 - League Tables, Fixture Simulation, Cup Simulation, and Player Match Result Flow

Overview
The goal of this epic is to start simulating the wider football world around the player.

This epic introduces:
- league tables on the calendar page
- simulation of all non-player fixtures on match days
- updating league tables from results
- season-end promotion/relegation/playoff handling
- simulation of cup fixtures and cup progression
- a Cups page to inspect cup fixtures and results
- a temporary player match resolution page for debugging so the world can progress correctly

The user's actual playable match engine is still out of scope.
For the player's own scheduled matches, this epic will use a temporary debug result-entry flow.

This epic is about:
- league structure progression
- fixture result simulation
- standings updates
- cup progression
- season-end placement outcomes
- temporary user match-result handling

This epic is not yet about:
- full playable match engine
- minute-by-minute simulation
- tactical UI inside matches
- advanced score modelling
- live match presentation
- transfers or squad changes between fixtures

================================================================
Core World Simulation Goal
================================================================

Now that teams and managers exist, the game should simulate the rest of the football world as time passes.

On match days:
- AI matches should be simulated

For the user:
- if it is not the user team's match day, the career continues normally
- if it is the user team's match day, progression should route to a temporary match-result page instead of simply advancing the day

================================================================
Match Simulation Inputs
================================================================

To simulate a match, each team needs these values:

- Team Overall Value (OV)
- Team Defence Tactic Rating (DTR)
- Team Attack Tactic Rating (ATR)
- Team Tactic Compatibility (TC)

The team's Match Rating is calculated as:

Match Rating = (OV * 2) + DTR + ATR + TC

This produces a value from 0 to 500.

For each match:
- calculate both teams' Match Rating
- compare them
- calculate the Match Rating Difference (MRD)

MRD is then used in the result-type matrix below.

================================================================
Match Result Type Matrix
================================================================

Use the exact matrix below.

Difference of	Lower Rating Team Big Win	Lower Rating Team Win	Draw	Higher Rating Team Win	Higher Rating Team Big Team Win
0-10	5	30	30	30	5
10-50	5	25	30	35	5
50-100	2	18	20	50	10
100-150	0.2	5.3	9.5	70	15
150-300	0.1	1.8	5.1	75	18
300+	0	0.8	2.8	56.4	40

Interpretation:
- determine which team has the higher Match Rating
- determine the MRD band
- roll against the probabilities for that band
- outcome determines:
  - lower-rated team big win
  - lower-rated team win
  - draw
  - higher-rated team win
  - higher-rated team big win

Result definitions:
- normal win = winning team scores 2 goals or fewer
- big win = winning team wins by 3 goals or more

This epic should generate a sensible final scoreline consistent with the chosen result type.

================================================================
Season-End League Outcome Rules
================================================================

When all league matches for the season are complete, apply the promotion/relegation/playoff structure.

League 5
- top team promoted automatically
- 2nd vs 3rd playoff
- playoff winner promoted

League 4
- top team promoted automatically
- 2nd vs 3rd playoff
- playoff winner promoted
- bottom 2 relegated

League 3
- top team promoted automatically
- 2nd vs 3rd playoff
- playoff winner promoted
- bottom 2 relegated

League 2
- top team promoted automatically
- 2nd vs 3rd playoff
- playoff winner promoted
- bottom 2 relegated

League 1
- bottom 2 relegated
- no promotion above League 1
- top 4 qualify for the Champions Cup next season

For this epic, these outcomes should be calculated and stored at season end.
Where future-season league regeneration depends on them later, store the outcome structure cleanly now.

================================================================
Cup Competition Requirements
================================================================

Cup matches must also be simulated using the same match-rating-based result system.

----------------------------------------------------------------
League Cup
----------------------------------------------------------------

Structure:
- all teams from Leagues 4 to 1
- total 32 teams
- 5 round knockout tournament

The tournament should support:
- fixtures
- results
- progression to next round

----------------------------------------------------------------
Champions Cup
----------------------------------------------------------------

Structure:
- top 4 teams from League 1 from the previous season
- plus all 12 foreign teams
- total 16 teams

Group stage:
- 4 groups of 4
- groups should be random
- the 4 League 1 teams must be spread across the 4 groups so none of them meet in the group stage

Knockout qualification:
- winner and runner-up from each group qualify

Quarter-finals:
- QF1 = Winner Group A vs Runner-up Group D
- QF2 = Winner Group B vs Runner-up Group C
- QF3 = Winner Group C vs Runner-up Group B
- QF4 = Winner Group D vs Runner-up Group A

Semi-finals:
- drawn at random from the quarter-final winners
- home team / ordering randomised

Final:
- drawn at random from the semi-final winners
- home team / ordering randomised

This structure must be simulated and advanced through the season calendar.

- show the details of the match
- show the teams involved
- provide 3 buttons:
  - Home Win
  - Draw

When one of those buttons is clicked:
- generate a random scoreline consistent with that chosen result type
- save it as the official result of the player's match
- update all relevant league/cup data
- return the user back into the career flow

This is a temporary debug resolution path only.

FEAT-1022 - Add Team Form and Home Advantage to Match Simulation
----------------------------------------------------------------

Description
Add team form and home advantage as additional factors in match simulation.

Team form is based on the results of the last 5 matches a team has played.
It should be stored as a rolling array and used to slightly adjust match outcome probabilities when simulating a match.

Home advantage gives the home team a small additional likelihood of winning.

This feature adds realistic momentum and home-field advantage to the match simulation system.

Requirements
- Every team must have a form array.
- The form array must represent the last 5 matches that team has played.
- Store the form as a rolling 5-result sequence.
- Results only need to distinguish wins for this feature, but the array should still represent real recent results clearly.
- Example form arrays:
  - WWLDW
  - WLDDD
- The array should always reflect the most recent 5 matches once enough matches have been played.

Form interpretation
- Team form strength is measured by the number of wins in the last 5 matches.
- Example:
  - WWLDW = 3 wins
  - WLDDD = 1 win
- In this case the first team has 2 more wins in form and should gain a match simulation advantage.

Form effect on match simulation
- When simulating a match, compare the number of wins in each team's form array.
- Calculate the difference in number of wins.
- Use that difference to adjust the match result likelihoods in favour of the team with the better form.

Use this exact mapping:
- Same wins in form array:
  - no change
- One team has 1 more win:
  - that team is 1% more likely to win the match
- One team has 2 more wins:
  - that team is 4% more likely to win the match
- One team has 3 more wins:
  - that team is 7% more likely to win the match
- One team has 4 or more wins:
  - that team is 10% more likely to win the match

Home advantage
- The home team should receive a 2% increased chance of winning the match.
- This modifier is applied in addition to the form modifier.
- This advantage should only apply to the home team.

Implementation guidance
- Apply the form modifier and home advantage modifier after the base match result chances have been determined from Match Rating Difference.
- The team with stronger form gains the form-based advantage.
- The home team gains the home advantage modifier.
- Ensure the total probability distribution remains valid after applying both adjustments.
- Keep the probability adjustment logic centralised and reusable.

Form storage and updating
- Every team should carry a form array in persistent career state.
- After each completed match, update that team's form array.
- Keep only the most recent 5 results.
- Once a team has played at least 5 matches, it should always have a full rolling 5-match form array.
- Form must carry over from season to season.
- Do not reset team form automatically at the start of a new season.

Foreign teams
- Foreign teams should start with a randomly generated form array at career creation.
- This is because they do not begin with a domestic league schedule generating their form.
- After initial generation, their form should then be updated based on their Champions Cup matches.

Domestic teams
- Domestic teams should build and maintain form through their played matches.
- After the first 5 matches in the game, all domestic teams should have a full form array all the time.

Scope notes
- This feature is about form storage and its effect on match simulation probability.
- It is not about displaying form in all UI yet unless useful for debugging.
- It should, however, be stored and accessible for future use in tables, fixtures, and match previews.

Acceptance Criteria
- Every team has a form array in career state.
- Form is stored as a rolling last-5-match result sequence.
- Match simulation compares the number of wins in each team’s form array.
- The exact form difference mapping is implemented:
  - 0 difference = 0%
  - 1 difference = 1%
  - 2 difference = 4%
  - 3 difference = 7%
  - 4+ difference = 10%
- The team with better form receives the corresponding increased chance to win.
- The home team receives an additional 2% win probability advantage.
- Form updates after every completed match.
- Form persists across seasons rather than resetting.
- Foreign teams begin with a randomly generated form array.
- After their initial form is created, foreign team form updates from Champions Cup matches.
- The implementation is centralised and reusable within the match simulation system.
