EPIC-021 - Training Card Usage, Training Screen, and First Team Growth

Overview
The goal of this epic is to implement how Training cards actually work in the game.

Training has 2 goals:
- primary goal: improve the current ratings of players in the first team
- secondary goal: reveal the potential value of first-team players whose potential is still hidden

This epic introduces:
- using Training cards from Career Home
- assigning a coach to run a training session
- temporary coach inUse state for the day
- a dedicated Training screen
- player focus/inspection during training
- sub-ratings for first-team players
- training success and failure logic
- potential reveal during training
- end-of-session reporting

This epic is about:
- training-card execution
- first-team player growth
- first-team potential reveal
- coach-driven training outcomes
- sub-rating progression

This epic is not about:
- scheduled training on the calendar
- academy training cards
- permanent coach fatigue systems beyond same-day inUse
- changing how Training cards are generated
- match engine behaviour

Core Training Card Usage Rules
================================================================

On Career Home:
- all Training cards should display a button:
  - Start Session

When Start Session is clicked:
- the user must choose an available coach
- that chosen coach is then marked:
  - inUse
- that inUse state applies for that day
- once the coach is chosen, navigate to the Training screen

The user can use as many Training cards as they want per day, provided:
- they have an available coach to assign to each one

A coach marked inUse for that day:
- cannot be used for another training session that same day

1. Top Section

================================================================
Potential Reveal During Training
================================================================

Training can reveal hidden potential values for first-team players.

This is separate from rating growth.

Process:
- take the training coach's Judgement rating
- divide it by 10
- round to 1 decimal place
- this becomes the chance that a player's potential is unhidden during that session

Example:
- coach Judgement = 42
- reveal chance = 4.2%

Rare card bonus:
- if the Training card used is Rare:
  - add 5% to this chance

If the reveal succeeds:
- pick 1 random player from the players involved in the training session
- unhide that player's potential
- show this in the end-of-session modal

Rules:
- only players involved in the training session are eligible
- if all involved players already have revealed potential, no new reveal needs to occur
- revealed potential remains permanently visible from then on

================================================================
Player Eligibility for Training Rewards
================================================================

The first stage of training growth is to determine which players are in the session and whether their potential gap allows them to benefit.

Use:
- the Positions field from the training card
- the player's current team position from Team Management

Positions field determines who is included in the training session.

Then for each player in the session:
- use:
  - Overall Rating (OR)
  - Potential Rating (PR)

Apply these exact rules:

If (PR - OR) > 5
- no effect
- move on from this part

Else If (PR - OR) > 2
- 20% chance the training will not affect this player no matter what
- if they fail here, they are treated as not taking part from this point onwards
- this should be flagged by the coach in the end-of-session modal

Else If (PR - OR) > 0
- 50% chance the training will not affect this player no matter what
- if they fail here, they are treated as not taking part from this point onwards
- this should be flagged by the coach in the end-of-session modal

Else If (PR - OR) = 0
- 0% chance the training can be successful
- this should be shown in the end-of-session modal about this player

Only players who remain eligible after this stage proceed to the training success stage.

================================================================
Training Success Rules
================================================================

For each eligible player:
- look at the training Type from the training card
- use the coach's rating for that training type
- this rating is the % chance training is successful for that player

Example:
- DF training card
- coach DF Training rating = 63
- each eligible player in the session gets a 63% success check

Each player is checked independently.

If successful:
- use the player's actual position column from the training card matrix
- grant the listed sub-rating points for that player's position

================================================================
Sub-Ratings System
================================================================

This epic introduces sub-ratings for first-team players.

Sub-ratings are tracked per skill.

Purpose:
- sub-ratings are the progress points needed to increase a visible rating by 1

Rule:
- to improve a visible rating from N to N+1
- the player needs N sub-rating points in that skill

Example:
- player Shooting = 22
- they need 22 Shooting sub-rating points to increase Shooting to 23

Important rule:
- sub-rating points do not carry over to the next level
- if the threshold is met or exceeded:
  - increase the visible rating by 1
  - reset that skill's sub-rating progress to 0
- do not carry excess progress into the next rating level

This system should be tracked for all trainable first-team player skills.

Training session gains:
- the values from the Training card matrix are sub-rating points granted to successful players for their position

All improvements to visible ratings and sub-ratings should be shown in the end-of-session modal.

================================================================
End-of-Session Modal
================================================================

After a training session is resolved:
- show an end-of-session modal

This modal should show:
- everything that happened to each player involved in the session

At minimum this includes:
- whether they were included in the session
- whether they were excluded by the OR/PR gating rules
- whether the coach flagged them as slowing/not learning
- whether training succeeded for them
- which sub-rating points were gained
- whether any visible rating increased
- whether a player's potential was revealed

This modal should also include:
- a button to end the training session
- clicking it returns the user to Career Home
