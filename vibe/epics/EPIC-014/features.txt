EPIC-014 - Roguelike Card System and Card Library Foundation

Overview
The goal of this epic is to replace the reverted training-first approach with the new roguelike card structure for the game.

There is no overall progression layer outside the current career. Progress in a run comes from the luck and quality of procedurally generated items and opportunities offered during that career.

This epic introduces:
- the core card system
- post-match card rewards
- the card library
- card rerolling by discarding cards
- the 4 card types:
  - Training
  - Scouting
  - Academy
  - Staff
- procedurally generated staff member cards
- fixed staff upgrade cards
- card rarity handling
- card colours by type
- debug tools to manually trigger card rewards

This epic is only about:
- generating cards
- offering cards
- adding chosen cards to the player's library
- displaying and managing the card library
- defining the exact data structures and reward rules

This epic is not yet about:
- using training cards
- using scouting cards
- using academy cards
- using staff upgrade cards
- academy gameplay resolution
- scouting gameplay resolution
- youth promotion gameplay resolution
- coach hiring effects from cards
- money/cost systems
- permanent progression between careers

================================================================
Core Roguelike Structure
================================================================

The game loop should now start building a roguelike reward structure around cards.

Cards are awarded after every match.

The user must choose 1 card from 3 offered cards before returning to the calendar.

Those cards are generated individually from the reward matrix in this epic.

The chosen card is added to the player's card library.

The player may also reroll the offered 3 cards by discarding 1 card from their existing card library.
- 1 discarded card = 1 reroll
- the rerolled choice still results in the user choosing 1 of the new 3 cards
- the chosen card is still added to the library

The library contains all card types together in one place, but it must be sortable and filterable.

================================================================
Card Types
================================================================

There are 4 top-level card types:

- Training
- Scouting
- Academy
- Staff

Card colours:
- Training Cards = blue
- Scouting Cards = yellow
- Academy Cards = orange
- Staff Cards = red

Staff cards have 2 internal sub-types:
- Staff Member
- Staff Upgrade

For staff cards:
- it is always a 50/50 split whether the generated staff card is:
  - a staff member card
  - a staff upgrade card

================================================================
Card Reward Timing
================================================================

Card reward choices happen at the end of every match.

This means:
- after the player's match result flow is resolved
- before the player returns to the calendar
- the player must choose 1 of the 3 cards offered
- the card choice step is mandatory unless using a reroll flow before choosing

The 3 offered cards:
- are generated independently
- can all be different
- can contain duplicates by type
- can contain 2 or even 3 of the same card type if the random rolls produce that result

There are no restrictions forcing one of each type in the offer.

================================================================
Card Library
================================================================

The player has one card library containing all card types.

The library should:
- live under the calendar
- be the same width as the calendar
- be horizontally scrolling
- support sorting
- support filtering

Sorting should at least be capable of handling:
- card type
- rarity
- name

Filtering should at least be capable of handling:
- Training
- Scouting
- Academy
- Staff
- rarity

The library must also support card removal:
- the user should be able to remove/discard cards from the library
- discarding is used for rerolls
- discarded cards are removed from the library completely

Cards should remain in the library until:
- discarded
- consumed by future systems
- explicitly removed by a future feature

For this epic, card functionality is not executed; only stored, displayed, filtered, sorted, and discardable.

================================================================
Post-Match Card Choice Flow
================================================================

After every match, the player must choose 1 of 3 offered cards before returning to the calendar.

Flow:
1. determine correct reward row using:
   - player's current league
   - player's current form wins
   - player's last match result
2. generate 3 offered cards independently
3. show them to the player
4. player chooses 1
5. chosen card added to library
6. then player can return to the calendar

Reroll flow:
- before choosing a card, the user may reroll the 3 offered cards
- to do this they must discard 1 card from their existing library
- 1 discarded card = 1 reroll
- after rerolling, a fresh set of 3 cards is generated from the same reward context
- the user then chooses 1 of those

Important:
- rerolls are optional
- cards used for rerolls must come from the library, not the current offered 3 unless you explicitly design that later
- a reroll still ends with exactly 1 chosen reward card being added to the library

================================================================
Debug Requirements
================================================================

For debugging, add a button in the debug section of the calendar that manually triggers a card choice.

Requirements:
- pressing the debug button should generate a normal 3-card reward choice flow
- choosing a card from this debug flow should still add the chosen card to the library
- this should not bypass the normal library add flow
- keep the debug trigger clearly separated from the normal match reward flow

FEAT-1401 - Define Unified Card Data Model
----------------------------------------------------------------

Description
Create the core shared card model used across all card types in the game.

Requirements
- Define a reusable card data model that can represent:
  - Training cards
  - Scouting cards
  - Academy cards
  - Staff Member cards
  - Staff Upgrade cards
- Include shared fields such as:
  - id
  - name
  - type
  - rarity
  - colour
  - subtype where relevant
- Support card-specific payloads for each card type.
- Keep the structure future-proof for when cards begin doing things later.

Acceptance Criteria
- A shared card model exists.
- All 4 top-level card types can be represented.
- Staff sub-types can also be represented cleanly.

FEAT-1408 - Implement 50/50 Staff Card Sub-Type Split
----------------------------------------------------------------

Description
When a Staff card reward is generated, determine whether it is a Staff Member or Staff Upgrade card.

Requirements
- Every Staff card reward must first resolve to:
  - Staff Member
  - or Staff Upgrade
- This split must be 50/50.
- If Staff Upgrade is selected, its rarity must match the originally rolled Staff rarity.
- If Staff Member is selected, its generated rarity must match the originally rolled Staff rarity.

Acceptance Criteria
- Staff rewards resolve to Member or Upgrade at a 50/50 split.
- The originally rolled rarity is preserved correctly.

----------------------------------------------------------------

  - player's current league
  - player's current form wins
  - player's last match result
- Look up the correct row from the reward matrix.
- Resolve one card result using the percentage values in that row.
- Result must include:
  - card type
  - rarity
- This resolver should be reusable because the 3 offered cards are generated independently.

Acceptance Criteria

- The correct reward row is used.
- The result identifies the correct type/rarity bucket.

----------------------------------------------------------------

Requirements

- There are no restrictions on duplicate types.
- The 3 cards may be:
  - all different
  - 2 of the same type
  - 3 of the same type
- Once type/rarity is rolled, instantiate the actual card:
  - Training from fixed definitions
  - Scouting from fixed definitions
  - Academy from fixed definitions
  - Staff via the 50/50 subtype rule

Acceptance Criteria
- 3 cards are offered after each match.
- Each card is generated independently.
- Offered cards can legitimately duplicate by type or rarity.

FEAT-1416 - Add Reroll System Using Library Card Discard
----------------------------------------------------------------

Description
Allow the user to reroll the 3 offered post-match cards by discarding 1 card from their library.

Requirements
- During the reward choice flow, the user may reroll the offered 3 cards.
- To reroll, the user must discard exactly 1 card from their existing library.
- 1 discarded card = 1 reroll.
- Rerolling generates a fresh set of 3 cards using the same reward context.
- The user must still choose 1 card from the rerolled set.
- Keep the reroll flow clear and explicit.

Acceptance Criteria
- The user can discard 1 library card to reroll.
- Reroll generates a fresh 3-card offer.
- The chosen rerolled card is added to the library.
- The discarded library card is removed permanently.

Epic Completion Criteria
================================================================

This epic is complete when:

- The full reward matrix has been implemented exactly as provided.
- Training, Scouting, Academy, and Staff card systems exist as card data/reward systems.
- Training, Scouting, Academy, and Staff Upgrade card definition tables are stored exactly as provided.
- Staff cards resolve 50/50 into Staff Member or Staff Upgrade cards.
- Staff Member cards are procedurally generated using the exact rarity/role-preference table.
- After every match, the player is offered 3 independently generated cards.
- The player must choose 1 card before returning to the calendar.
- The chosen card is added to the card library.
- The player can reroll the 3-card offer by discarding 1 card from the library.
- The card library appears under the calendar, matches the calendar width, and scrolls horizontally.
- The library is sortable and filterable.
- Cards can be discarded from the library.
- A debug button exists to manually trigger a card choice.
- The chosen debug reward also adds correctly to the card library.
- Card effects themselves are not yet executed; only the card foundation and reward flow are implemented.
